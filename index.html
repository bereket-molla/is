About

I was born and raised in Ethiopia 🇪🇹, and those were some of the best years of my life.

My first brush with computer science came in 2016 with a humble Visual Basic class. Back then, programming felt like magic spells on a screen. Over time I've come to see it more as a mix of math, more math (and some magic spells :) ).

These days I work at Amazon on the Promise Optimization team — the folks behind that delivery date you see when you order something. Before that, I interned twice in Fulfillment Optimization, all while studying computer science at Columbia University, mostly diving into systems programming with some machine learning on the side.

Lately, I've been exploring the mathematical foundations of robotic grasping through Why Linearity Shows Up in Grasps—an investigation into why linear dimensionality reduction works so well for controlling complex robotic hands.
